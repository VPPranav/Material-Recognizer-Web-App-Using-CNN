Description

Glass is a non-crystalline, often transparent solid made from silica (sand), sodium carbonate, and calcium carbonate. It's known for its clarity, versatility, and recyclability.

Glass is 100% recyclable and can be recycled endlessly without loss in quality or purity. It takes approximately 1 million years to decompose naturally.

Common Uses

Containers, windows, tableware, decorative items, laboratory equipment, and optical components.

Description

Paper is a thin material produced by pressing together moist fibers of cellulose pulp derived from wood, rags, or grasses, and drying them into flexible sheets.

Common Uses

Writing, printing, packaging, cleaning, and various industrial and construction processes.
